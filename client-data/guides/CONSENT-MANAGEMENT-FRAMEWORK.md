## Overview

This framework ensures **ethical, legal, and privacy-compliant** handling of all client data in the SwanStudios Personal Training System. It provides clear protocols for obtaining, storing, managing, and honoring client consent across all data types and use cases.

### Why This Matters
- **Legal Protection:** Protects SwanStudios from liability
- **Client Trust:** Demonstrates respect for client autonomy
- **Privacy Compliance:** Meets GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA requirements
- **Ethical Standards:** Upholds professional trainer-client relationships
- **Data Governance:** Establishes clear data ownership and usage rights

### Core Principles
1. **Informed Consent:** Clients understand what they're consenting to
2. **Granular Control:** Clients can consent to specific uses, not just "all or nothing"
3. **Easy Withdrawal:** Clients can withdraw consent at any time
4. **Transparent Usage:** Clients know how their data is being used
5. **Minimal Collection:** Only collect data necessary for training

## Legal & Ethical Foundation

### Applicable Laws & Regulations

#### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
**Applies if:** You transmit health information electronically to health plans or clearinghouses
**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain written authorization for use/disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- Provide Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP)
- Allow clients to access, amend, and receive accounting of disclosures

**SwanStudios Status:** As a personal trainer, you likely do NOT fall under HIPAA as a "Covered Entity" UNLESS you bill insurance or transmit PHI electronically to covered entities. However, following HIPAA-like standards is considered best practice.

#### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
**Applies if:** You have clients in the EU/EEA
**Key Requirements:**
- Lawful basis for processing (usually: consent or contractual necessity)
- Right to access, rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Data portability
- Explicit consent for special category data (health data)
- Data breach notification within 72 hours

#### CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
**Applies if:** You have clients in California and meet revenue/data thresholds
**Key Requirements:**
- Disclose data collection practices
- Allow opt-out of data sales
- Right to deletion
- Right to know what data is collected

#### State Personal Training Regulations
- Many states require liability waivers
- Some require medical clearance for high-risk clients
- Professional liability insurance often mandates written consent

### Professional Standards
- **NASM Code of Ethics:** Maintain client confidentiality, obtain informed consent
- **ACE Code of Ethics:** Respect client privacy, secure written agreements
- **NSCA Professional Standards:** Protect client information, obtain medical clearance when needed

## Consent Types & Requirements

### 1. General Training Services Consent
**Required for:** All clients
**Purpose:** Agreement to participate in personal training services
**Covers:**
- Training program participation
- Physical assessment and baseline testing
- Exercise instruction and supervision
- Nutrition guidance (if offered)
- Progress tracking and documentation

**Sample Language:**
```
I, [Client Name], voluntarily consent to participate in personal training services
provided by SwanStudios Personal Training. I understand that:

1. Physical exercise involves inherent risks including injury, cardiovascular events,
   and in rare cases, death.
2. I have disclosed all medical conditions, injuries, and limitations to my trainer.
3. I will immediately inform my trainer of any discomfort, pain, or unusual symptoms.
4. I am responsible for following my doctor's advice regarding exercise participation.
5. I can stop any exercise at any time if I feel unsafe or uncomfortable.

Signature: _________________ Date: _________________
```

### 2. Medical Information Consent
**Required for:** All clients providing health/medical data
**Purpose:** Authorization to collect, store, and use health information
**Covers:**
- Medical history (conditions, surgeries, medications)
- Injury history and current pain
- Doctor clearance status
- Biometric data (weight, body fat %, blood pressure, etc.)
- Physical limitations and contraindications

**Sample Language:**
```
I authorize SwanStudios Personal Training to collect, store, and use my medical and
health information for the sole purpose of designing safe and effective training programs.

I understand:
1. My health information will be stored securely and not shared without my consent.
2. My trainer is NOT a medical professional and cannot diagnose or treat conditions.
3. I should consult my doctor before starting any new exercise program.
4. I have the right to request deletion of my health information at any time.

Medical conditions I am disclosing: _________________________________

Signature: _________________ Date: _________________
```

## Consent Collection Workflow

### Phase 1: Initial Consultation (Pre-Training)

**Step 1: Provide Information**
- Give client a **Consent Information Packet** that includes:
  - Overview of data collection practices
  - Types of data collected
  - How data will be used and stored
  - Client rights (access, deletion, withdrawal)
  - Privacy and security measures
  - Contact information for questions

**Step 2: Review Consent Forms**
- Walk through each consent form with the client
- Answer questions and clarify any confusion
- Emphasize that consents are **optional** where applicable
- Do NOT pressure client to consent to marketing use

**Step 3: Collect Signed Consents**
- Client signs physical or digital consent forms
- Trainer signs as witness
- Date all forms
- Provide client with copy of signed forms

**Step 4: Store Consents Securely**
- Scan physical forms to PDF
- Store in encrypted folder (see SECURITY-PRIVACY-IMPLEMENTATION.md)
- Add consent record to `client-data/[client-folder]/consent/`
- Update CLIENT-REGISTRY.md with consent status

### Phase 2: Ongoing Consent Management

**Quarterly Consent Review**
- Every 3 months, ask client: "Has anything changed with your consent preferences?"
- Document the review date in `consent/consent-review-log.md`

**Annual Consent Renewal**
- Once per year, have client re-sign key consents
- Update any changes to privacy policies or data practices
- Refresh client understanding of their rights

**Event-Triggered Consent**
- Before any NEW use of data (e.g., posting testimonial), obtain specific consent
- Before sharing data with new third party, obtain authorization
- If AI tools change significantly, re-consent to AI usage

## Consent Withdrawal Process

### Client Rights
Clients have the **absolute right** to:
1. Withdraw any consent at any time
2. Request deletion of their data
3. Request a copy of all their data
4. Request correction of inaccurate data
5. Opt out of specific data uses (e.g., AI coaching)

### Withdrawal Workflow

**Step 1: Client Requests Withdrawal**
- Client can request via:
  - In-person conversation
  - Email
  - Text message
  - Phone call

**Step 2: Document the Request**
- Create entry in `consent/withdrawal-requests.md`:
  ```markdown
  ## Withdrawal Request: [Date]
  **Consent Type:** Marketing use of photos
  **Request Date:** 2025-03-10
  **Request Method:** Email
  **Action Taken:** Removed all client photos from website and social media
  **Completion Date:** 2025-03-12
  **Client Notified:** 2025-03-12 via email
  ```

**Step 3: Take Immediate Action**
- **Marketing Consent:** Remove photos/testimonials from all platforms within 48 hours
- **AI Coaching Consent:** Disable AI features, switch to human-only check-ins
- **Data Sharing Consent:** Disconnect third-party integrations
- **Full Data Deletion:** See Data Deletion Workflow below

**Step 4: Confirm with Client**
- Send written confirmation of action taken
- Example email:
  ```
  Subject: Consent Withdrawal Confirmation

  Hi [Client Name],

  This confirms that I have removed all photos of you from:
  - SwanStudios website (removed 3/12/2025)
  - Instagram (removed 3/12/2025)
  - Facebook (removed 3/12/2025)

  Your photos remain in your secure personal progress folder for your use only.

  Thank you for letting me know your preference changed. Please reach out
  anytime if you have questions.

  Best,
  [Trainer Name]
  ```

## HIPAA Compliance

### When HIPAA Applies
HIPAA applies to personal trainers ONLY if you are a **Covered Entity** or **Business Associate**:
- **Covered Entity:** Healthcare providers who transmit health info electronically (e.g., billing insurance)
- **Business Associate:** Work with a covered entity and handle PHI on their behalf

**Most personal trainers do NOT fall under HIPAA.**

However, following HIPAA-like standards is **best practice** and provides strong legal protection.

### HIPAA-Like Standards for Personal Trainers

#### 1. Minimum Necessary Rule
- Collect only the health information necessary for training
- Don't ask for full medical records if not needed
- Example: Ask "Do you have knee pain?" not "Send me your orthopedic surgeon's notes"

#### 2. Use and Disclosure Limitations
- Use health information ONLY for:
  - Designing safe training programs
  - Monitoring progress
  - Coordinating with client's doctor (with consent)
- Do NOT:
  - Share client health info with other clients
  - Discuss client health issues publicly
  - Use health info for marketing without explicit consent

#### 3. Secure Storage
- Encrypt digital health records
- Lock physical files in secure location
- Use password-protected devices
- No health info in unencrypted emails or texts

#### 4. Access Controls
- Only trainer has access to client health information
- Log any access by third parties (e.g., if you hire an assistant)
- Client can request access log

#### 5. Breach Notification
- If health data is breached (lost, stolen, hacked):
  - Notify client within 48-72 hours
  - Explain what happened and what you're doing to fix it
  - Offer credit monitoring if SSN or financial info was exposed
  - Document the breach and response

## Minor Client Considerations

### Legal Requirements
- **Parental Consent:** Required for all clients under 18
- **Age of Consent:** Varies by state (usually 18, sometimes 16 for medical decisions)
- **Medical Clearance:** STRONGLY recommended for minors, especially under 14

### Special Protections for Minors

#### 1. Parental Involvement
- Parent/guardian must sign all consent forms
- Parent should be present for initial consultation
- Parent receives copies of progress reports
- Parent can access all client data at any time

#### 2. Privacy Considerations
- Adolescents (14-17) may want some privacy from parents
- Balance minor's autonomy with parental rights
- Example: Teen can choose not to share weight with parents, but trainer should encourage transparency

#### 3. Photo/Video Restrictions
- EXTRA caution with minor photos
- Parental consent required for ANY photo/video
- NEVER post minor photos on social media without explicit parental permission
- Consider "no face" policy for minors in marketing

#### 4. AI Coaching Restrictions
- Limit AI coaching for minors under 16
- Parental oversight of AI check-in content
- No unsupervised AI communication with minors

## Data Retention Policies

### Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Reason |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Signed consent forms | 7 years after last service | Legal protection (statute of limitations) |
| Liability waivers | 7 years after last service | Legal protection |
| Payment records | 7 years | Tax/accounting requirements |
| Training programs | 3 years after last service | Professional records |
| Progress photos | Until client requests deletion | Client property |
| Medical information | 3 years after last service | Professional records |
| Email/text communications | 1 year | Business records |
| AI coaching data | Until client requests deletion | Service provision |

### Retention Workflow

**Active Client:**
- Retain all data for duration of client relationship
- Regular backups
- Quarterly consent reviews

**Inactive Client (No sessions in 6+ months):**
- Send email: "We haven't trained in 6 months. Do you want to keep your data active or delete it?"
- If no response in 30 days, move to "Inactive Archive"
- Retain consent forms and legal docs for 7 years
- Delete progress photos and non-essential data after 3 years

**Deleted Account:**
- Delete all training data
- Retain ONLY: consent forms, waivers, payment records
- Archive in "Legal Archive" folder
- Delete after 7 years

## AI Coaching Consent

### Specific AI Disclosures

Clients must be informed of:
1. **What AI does:** "AI sends daily check-in messages and analyzes your progress data"
2. **AI limitations:** "AI cannot diagnose injuries or medical conditions"
3. **Human oversight:** "All AI recommendations are reviewed by your human trainer"
4. **Third-party processing:** "Your data may be processed by OpenAI (ChatGPT API)"
5. **Data privacy:** "AI providers have strict data privacy agreements"
6. **Opt-out:** "You can opt out of AI coaching at any time"

### AI Data Processing Agreement

When using third-party AI services (OpenAI, etc.), ensure:
- AI provider has Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- AI provider does NOT use client data to train models (OpenAI API has this protection)
- AI provider encrypts data in transit and at rest
- AI provider deletes data after processing (if possible)

### AI Consent Granularity

Allow clients to consent to specific AI features:
- [ ] Daily text check-ins
- [ ] Voice check-ins
- [ ] AI analysis of progress photos
- [ ] AI workout adjustments
- [ ] AI nutrition insights
- [ ] AI pain tracking

Client can mix and match (e.g., "Yes to text check-ins, no to photo analysis").

## Photo & Video Consent

### Types of Visual Media

1. **Progress Photos (Private)**
   - Front, side, back poses
   - Clothing: Athletic wear (sports bra/shorts or similar)

2. **Form Analysis Videos (Private)**
   - Video of exercise technique
   - Purpose: Identify form issues
   - Stored in encrypted folder
   - Deleted after analysis (or retained per client preference)

3. **Transformation Photos (Marketing)**
   - Before/after comparisons
   - Used on website, social media, ads
   - Requires separate marketing consent
   - Client can revoke at any time

### Photography Best Practices

#### Before Taking Photos
- [ ] Confirm consent is signed
- [ ] Explain what photos will be used for
- [ ] Allow client to choose clothing
- [ ] Offer private space to change
- [ ] Ask "Are you comfortable with this?"

#### During Photo Session
- [ ] Use neutral, private location
- [ ] No other clients present
- [ ] Professional demeanor
- [ ] Show client the photos before saving
- [ ] Allow re-takes if client is uncomfortable

#### After Photo Session
- [ ] Transfer photos to encrypted storage immediately
- [ ] Delete from camera/phone after transfer
- [ ] Do NOT show photos to other clients
- [ ] Label files clearly: "[ClientName]-Progress-[Date].jpg"

## Third-Party Sharing

### Common Third-Party Integrations

1. **Wearable Devices (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin, Whoop, Oura)**
   - **Data shared TO trainer:** Steps, heart rate, sleep, workouts
   - **Consent required:** Yes, explicit consent for each device
   - **Revocation:** Client can disconnect at any time
   - **Privacy:** Review each provider's privacy policy with client

2. **Nutrition Apps (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, LoseIt)**
   - **Data shared TO trainer:** Food logs, macros, calories
   - **Consent required:** Yes
   - **Best practice:** Client shares manually (screenshot) rather than API integration

3. **Medical Providers (Doctors, Physical Therapists)**
   - **Data shared TO provider:** Training program, progress, concerns
   - **Data shared FROM provider:** Medical clearance, restrictions
   - **Consent required:** HIPAA authorization form
   - **Format:** Formal written request with client signature

4. **Payment Processors (Stripe, Square, PayPal)**
   - **Data shared:** Name, email, payment info
   - **Consent:** Implied by using service
   - **Privacy:** Covered by processor's privacy policy

## FAQs

### Q: What if a client verbally consents but won't sign a form?
**A:** Verbal consent is better than nothing, but not legally strong. Document it:
- Email follow-up: "Thanks for agreeing to [X] during our session today. Reply to confirm."
- Note in client file: "Client verbally consented to [X] on [date]."
- Best practice: Explain WHY the signature protects both of you and gently encourage signing.

### Q: Can I record client workouts for form analysis without consent?
**A:** NO. Always get consent before recording video, even for form analysis. Many states require two-party consent for recording.

### Q: What if a client requests deletion but owes money?
**A:** You can retain records necessary for the business dispute (invoices, payment history) but should still delete training data per their request. Consult a lawyer if unsure.

### Q: Do I need consent to send workout reminders via text?
**A:** Yes. Obtain consent for SMS communication. Include option to opt out (e.g., "Reply STOP to unsubscribe"). Comply with TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act).

### Q: What if I want to use a client's transformation for a case study?
**A:** Get explicit written consent that specifies:
- What data will be shared (photos, story, metrics)
- Where it will be published (website, ebook, conference presentation)
- Whether client will be identified or anonymous
- Client's right to revoke consent

### Q: How do I handle consent for clients who don't speak English well?
**A:** Provide consent forms in client's native language (use professional translation service). Use interpreter during consent discussion if needed. Document: "Consent forms provided in [language] and reviewed with interpreter."

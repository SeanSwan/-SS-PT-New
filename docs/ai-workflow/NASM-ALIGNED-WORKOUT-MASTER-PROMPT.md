## OPT™ Model Deep Dive

### Phase 1: Stabilization Endurance
**Goal**: Improve neuromuscular efficiency, stability, and control
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (foundation building)
**Acute Variables**:
- Reps: 12-20 per set
- Sets: 2-4 per exercise
- Tempo: 4/2/1 (4s eccentric, 2s isometric, 1s concentric)
- Rest: 60-90 seconds
- Intensity: 50-70% 1RM or RPE 4-6

**Key Characteristics**:
- Slow, controlled movements
- Emphasis on form and stability
- Unilateral and anti-rotational exercises
- Proprioceptive challenges (unstable surfaces)
- Core integration in every movement

**Progression Criteria**:
- Client demonstrates perfect form on all exercises
- Can maintain stability throughout full ROM
- No compensatory movements observed
- Client reports improved balance and control

### Phase 2: Strength Endurance
**Goal**: Improve muscular endurance while maintaining stability
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (endurance building)
**Acute Variables**:
- Reps: 8-12 per set
- Sets: 3-4 per exercise (often supersetted)
- Tempo: 2/1/1 or 3/1/1
- Rest: 45-60 seconds (short for metabolic stress)
- Intensity: 70-80% 1RM or RPE 6-8

**Key Characteristics**:
- Supersets: Strength exercise → Stabilization variant
- Moderate metabolic stress
- Combination of stable and unstable exercises
- Higher volume than Phase 1
- Introduction of plyometric elements

**Progression Criteria**:
- Client can complete all sets with good form
- Demonstrates improved work capacity
- Shows better stabilization under fatigue
- Ready for hypertrophy-focused training

### Phase 3: Muscular Development/Hypertrophy
**Goal**: Increase muscle size and definition
**Duration**: 6-8 weeks (muscle building)
**Acute Variables**:
- Reps: 6-12 per set
- Sets: 3-6 per exercise
- Tempo: 2/1/2 or 3/1/3
- Rest: 60-90 seconds
- Intensity: 75-85% 1RM or RPE 7-9

**Key Characteristics**:
- Higher volume training
- Moderate to high intensity
- Focus on time under tension
- Combination of compound and isolation movements
- Metabolic stress for hypertrophy

**Progression Criteria**:
- Measurable increases in muscle size/circumference
- Improved strength in hypertrophy rep ranges
- Client reports muscle fullness and definition
- Ready for maximal strength development

### Phase 4: Maximal Strength
**Goal**: Develop high-force production capabilities
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (strength peaking)
**Acute Variables**:
- Reps: 1-5 per set
- Sets: 4-6 per exercise
- Tempo: 3/1/1 or explosive concentric
- Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets
- Intensity: 85-100% 1RM or RPE 8-10

**Key Characteristics**:
- Heavy compound movements
- Low rep ranges with high intensity
- Longer recovery periods
- Focus on technique and force production
- Cluster sets for technique maintenance

**Progression Criteria**:
- Significant strength gains (5-10% improvement)
- Improved 1RM in major compound lifts
- Better force production capabilities
- Ready for power development

### Phase 5: Power
**Goal**: Maximize power output and explosiveness
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (power development)
**Acute Variables**:
- Reps: 1-5 heavy + 6-10 light/explosive
- Sets: 3-5 per exercise
- Tempo: Explosive concentric (X/X/X)
- Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets
- Intensity: 85-90% heavy, 30-50% light

**Key Characteristics**:
- Contrast training (heavy → light/explosive)
- Olympic lift variations
- Medicine ball throws
- Plyometric progressions
- High force + high velocity combinations

**Progression Criteria**:
- Improved vertical jump or throwing power
- Better sprint times or acceleration
- Enhanced reactive strength
- Peak performance capabilities achieved

## Corrective Exercise Continuum Integration

### NASM-CES Assessment Protocol

#### Step 1: Static Postural Assessment
**Areas to Evaluate**:
- Anterior/Posterior View: Head, shoulders, spine, pelvis, knees, ankles
- Lateral View: Head, shoulders, spine, pelvis, knees, ankles
- Overhead View: Shoulders, pelvis, spine alignment

**Common Compensations**:
- Forward head posture
- Rounded shoulders
- Anterior pelvic tilt
- Knee valgus/varus
- Excessive foot pronation/supination

#### Step 2: Movement Assessment
**Overhead Squat Assessment**:
- Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
- Arms overhead, observe full squat pattern
- Look for compensations in ankles, knees, hips, low back, shoulders

**Single-Leg Squat Assessment**:
- Test unilateral stability and strength
- Observe for pelvic drop, knee valgus, or trunk lean

**Pushing/Pulling Assessment**:
- Push-up or bench press form
- Observe shoulder positioning and scapular movement
- Pulling assessment (rows, pull-ups)

#### Step 3: Corrective Exercise Continuum Application

**Phase 1: Inhibit (Neuromuscular Inhibition)**
- Self-Myofascial Release (SMR) techniques
- Foam rolling, tennis ball, or roller massage
- Duration: 1-2 minutes per muscle group
- Focus: Tight/overactive muscles

**Phase 2: Lengthen (Neuromuscular Flexibility)**
- Static stretching of inhibited muscles
- Duration: 20-30 seconds per stretch
- Hold at first point of resistance
- Focus: Restore normal muscle length

**Phase 3: Activate (Neuromuscular Activation)**
- Isolated strengthening of weak/inhibited muscles
- Low load, high repetition (15-25 reps)
- Focus: Improve muscle activation patterns

**Phase 4: Integrate (Functional Movement Integration)**
- Multi-plane, multi-joint movements
- Incorporate corrected muscles into functional patterns
- Progress to sport-specific or daily activity movements

### Common Corrective Protocols

#### Upper Crossed Syndrome (UCS)
```
Inhibit: Upper trapezius, levator scapulae, pectoralis major/minor
Lengthen: Upper trapezius, levator scapulae, pectoralis major/minor
Activate: Lower trapezius, serratus anterior, rhomboids
Integrate: Rows, presses, overhead movements with proper scapular positioning
```

#### Lower Crossed Syndrome (LCS)
```
Inhibit: Hip flexors, erector spinae
Lengthen: Hip flexors, erector spinae
Activate: Gluteal muscles, transverse abdominis
Integrate: Squats, deadlifts, lunges with proper pelvic positioning
```

#### Pronation Distortion Syndrome (PDS)
```
Inhibit: Gastrocnemius, soleus, hip flexors
Lengthen: Gastrocnemius, soleus, hip flexors
Activate: Anterior tibialis, gluteus medius, posterior tibialis
Integrate: Single-leg balance, step-ups, lunges with neutral foot positioning
```

**Phase**: Phase 2: Strength Endurance
**Goal**: Full body strength endurance, posterior chain focus
**Session Length**: 60 minutes

---

### Corrective Warm-up (10 min)

**Inhibit (SMR)**:
- Adductors (inner thigh): 60s each side
- Pectoralis Minor: 60s each side

**Lengthen (Static Stretch)**:
- Adductor Stretch: 30s each side
- Pec Minor Stretch (doorway): 30s each side

**Activate**:
- Glute Bridge: 2x15
- Band Pull-Aparts: 2x20

**Integrate (Dynamic Warm-up)**:
- Bodyweight Squats (focus on knee tracking): 2x15
- Arm Circles & Leg Swings: 60s

---

### Main Workout (45 min)

**Block A: Lower Body Superset**
- A1: DB Goblet Squat (Strength): 3x10 @ RPE 7
- A2: Single-Leg Balance Reach (Stabilization): 3x12 each side
- *Rest: 60s after superset*

**Block B: Upper Body Superset**
- B1: Seated Cable Row (Strength): 3x10 @ RPE 8
- B2: TRX Row (Stabilization): 3x12 @ RPE 7
- *Rest: 60s after superset*

**Block C: Accessory Superset**
- C1: DB Romanian Deadlift (Strength): 3x12 @ RPE 7
- C2: Stability Ball Hamstring Curl (Stabilization): 3x15
- *Rest: 60s after superset*

**Block D: Core & Anti-Rotation**
- D1: Pallof Press (Cable): 3x12 each side
- D2: Plank with Alternating Leg Lift: 3x45s
- *Rest: 45s*

---

### Cool-down (5 min)

- Full Body Static Stretching: 30s each major muscle group
- Deep Breathing: 60s

---

### Rationale
This workout adheres to **Phase 2: Strength Endurance** by pairing a traditional strength exercise with a stabilization-focused exercise for each major muscle group. This improves muscular endurance, stability, and work capacity. The corrective warm-up addresses the client's knee valgus and shoulder elevation to improve movement quality before loading.
